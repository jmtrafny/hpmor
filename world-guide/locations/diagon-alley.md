# Diagon Alley

## Overview

Diagon Alley is magical Britain's primary shopping district, featuring "the twisting main street" with shops for magical supplies, Gringotts Bank, and access via the Leaky Cauldron [Ch. 3, 6]. Harry and McGonagall spend most of Chapter 6 shopping here for Hogwarts supplies.

## Access [EXPLICIT Ch. 3]

### Leaky Cauldron [Ch. 3]

- Pub serving as gateway between Muggle and magical London
- Located on Muggle street, but Muggles cannot perceive it
- McGonagall and Harry enter from Muggle side [Ch. 3]
- Leads to courtyard with brick wall
- Tapping specific bricks opens passage to Diagon Alley

### Recognition [Ch. 3]

"My grandson was an Auror...

" - elderly woman recognizes Harry as Boy-Who-Lived immediately upon entering [Ch. 3]
- Harry becomes object of attention: "Thank you, Harry Potter. Thank heavens for you" [Ch. 3]

## Layout [IMPLIED Ch. 6]

### Main Street [Ch. 6]

- "Twisting main street of Diagon Alley" [Ch. 6]
- Expensive shops display merchandise here
- Open-front shops with magical protections ("slight grey glows") [Ch. 6]
- Well-trafficked with shoppers
- Harry: "I, Harry James Potter-Evans-Verres, do now claim this territory in the name of Science" [Ch. 6]

### Side Alleys

- Smaller alleys branch off from main street
- "Alleyway between two shops which was paved in dirty bricks and dead-ended in a wall of solid black dirt" [Ch. 6]
- Less traveled, used for private conversations

## Major Establishments

### Gringotts Bank [EXPLICIT Ch. 3, 4]

**Description**: "Great white building with vast bronze doors, and carven words above saying Gringotts Bank" [Ch. 3]

**Staff**: Goblins run the bank [Ch. 4]

**Services**:
- Family vaults for storing gold and valuables
- Currency exchange (Muggle money to Galleons)
- High security ("guarded by every spell high and low that the goblins know") [Ch. 51]
- Despite this, "vaults have been successfully robbed" [Ch. 51]
- "For what wizardry can do, wizardry can undo" [Ch. 51]

**Potter Vault** [Ch. 4]:
- "Huge heaps of gold Galleons"
- Silver Sickles and bronze Knuts also present
- Family heirlooms
- James Potter was "last heir of an old family" so substantial inheritance [Ch. 4]
- Harry has vault key (later controlled by McGonagall/Dumbledore) [Ch. 24]

**Goblin Relations** [Ch. 15]:
- "Goblin nation is in a permanent state of war with all magical counterfeiters" [Ch. 15]
- "They will not send Aurors. They will send an army" [Ch. 15]
- Goblins have "ways of finding out" who counterfeits money

## HPMOR vs Canon Differences

**Retained from Canon**:
- Gringotts as goblin-run bank with high security
- Leaky Cauldron access point
- Main shopping street with standard shops (Ollivanders, Flourish and Blotts, Madam Malkin's)
- Potter family vault with substantial gold
- Knockturn Alley as darker connected area

**HPMOR Specific Details**:
- Harry's "claim this territory in the name of Science" moment [Ch. 6]
- Detailed Emergency Healing Pack description [Ch. 6]
- Emphasis on war's psychological impact on shopkeepers [Ch. 6]
- Goblin counterfeit warfare explicitly stated [Ch. 15]
- Trunk shop's elaborate decor described [Ch. 6]

**Tone Difference**:
- HPMOR emphasizes post-war trauma more than canon
- More detailed economics (prices, vault contents)
- Harry's scientific mindset applied to magical items (mokeskin pouch experiments)

## Implications for Fanfiction Writers

**What's Established**:
- Diagon Alley is main wizarding shopping district accessed via Leaky Cauldron
- Gringotts vaults have been successfully robbed despite high security
- War trauma affects shopkeepers and customers (salesgirl incident)
- Harry's Potter vault contains substantial gold (specific amount not stated)
- Emergency Healing Pack costs 5 Galleons
- Mokeskin pouches have Retrieval Charms with natural language understanding
- Goblin nation permanently at war with counterfeiters

**What's Open to Interpretation** [AMBIGUOUS]:
- Exact number and names of shops beyond those mentioned
- Full layout of Diagon Alley street system
- What "unfortunate incident at Potions shop" entailed [Ch. 6]
- How much gold Harry actually has in vault
- Details of Knockturn Alley beyond reputation

**Common Mistakes to Avoid**:
- Don't forget war's psychological impact on magical Britain
- Don't make Gringotts impenetrable - text says vaults have been robbed
- Don't ignore that goblins will send army after counterfeiters
- Don't forget this is ten years post-war, not immediately after
- Don't make Diagon Alley frivolous - serious undertones present

**Useful for Stories**:
- Shopping scenes for character bonding/conflict
- Gringotts as heist location (has been robbed before)
- War trauma as character background motivation
- Harry's vault as financial resource for plots
- Diagon Alley as public space where news spreads
- Goblin relations as political subplot
