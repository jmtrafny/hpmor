# Death Eaters

## Overview

The Death Eaters were followers of Lord Voldemort during the First Wizarding War (1970-1981). What began as a disposable practice identity became Voldemort's preferred persona: "glowing red eyes, pointlessly cruel to his underlings, pursuing a political agenda of naked personal ambition combined with blood purism as argued by drunks in Knockturn Alley" [Ch. 108]. After Voldemort's defeat, many Death Eaters avoided punishment through claims of Imperius or political influence.

## Formation and Origins [EXPLICIT]

### Voldemort's Explanation [Ch. 108]

**Creation as Practice Identity**:
- Created before David Monroe persona to "learn how the ropes worked, and get mistakes out of the way"
- First step toward becoming "a truly terrible Dark Lord for David Monroe to fight"
- "My first underlings were hired in a tavern, given cloaks and skull masks, and told to introduce themselves as Death Eaters" [Ch. 108]

**Evolution**:
- Started as deliberate caricature of Dark Lord
- Voldemort: "Eventually you realized you were just having more fun as Voldemort" [Ch. 108]
- Became primary identity, overshadowing "heroic" David Monroe persona
- Death Eater identity persisted even after strategic purpose ended

### Historical Context [Ch. 3]

**Timeline**: Approximately 1970-1981 (eleven years before HPMOR present)

**Public Perception**:
- "The Dark Lord had raged upon wizarding Britain like a wilding wolf, tearing and rending at the fabric of their everyday lives" [Ch. 3]
- "Death Eaters had followed in the Dark Lord's wake and in his vanguard, carrion vultures to pick at wounds, or snakes to bite and weaken" [Ch. 3]
- "Death Eaters wielded more than wands; there was wealth within those masked ranks, and political power, and secrets held in blackmail, to paralyse a society trying to protect itself" [Ch. 3]

## Structure and Membership

### Inner Circle [IMPLIED]

**Bellatrix Black** [Ch. 27, 51, 108]:
- Most devoted follower: "Literally the most evil person in the entire world" after Voldemort's defeat [Ch. 27]
- Tortured Frank and Alice Longbottom "into insanity because she wanted to find out what happened to the Dark Lord" [Ch. 27]
- Imprisoned in Azkaban without being under Imperius - "broken" through "torture, and Legilimency, and rituals of which I will not speak" [Ch. 51]
- Given special instructions: recover Voldemort's wand and take it to graveyard where his spirit would appear [Ch. 108]
- Later rescued by Quirrell and Harry [Ch. 55-57]

**Lucius Malfoy** [IMPLIED throughout]:
- Never imprisoned, maintained political power
- Avoided punishment (method not detailed, likely wealth/influence)
- Post-war maintains blood purist agenda through legal/political means
- Father of Draco, Lord of Noble and Most Ancient House

**Others Mentioned by Name**:
- **Lesath Lestrange's father**: Death Eater who died in war [Ch. 27]
- **Amycus Carrow**: "That one time just before Father told him to stop it, he wasn't the Dark Lord" [Ch. 21] - implies frightening behavior
- **Theodore Nott's father (Charles Nott)**: Implied Death Eater connections [Ch. 33]

### Regular Members [IMPLIED]

- Recruited from various blood purist sympathizers
- Given "cloaks and skull masks" [Ch. 108]
- Mixture of ideological believers and opportunists
- Wielded "wealth...and political power, and secrets held in blackmail" [Ch. 3]

### Post-War Status

**Those Imprisoned**:
- Bellatrix Black: Azkaban for torture of Longbottoms
- Others "a dozen or so" in Azkaban [IMPLIED by Ch. 55 breakout discussion]

**Those Who Avoided Punishment**:
- Many claimed Imperius Curse defense
- Wealthy/politically connected used influence
- Some like Lucius maintained power and respectability

## Dark Mark [EXPLICIT Ch. 51]

### Functionality

**Communication System**:
- Voldemort told Death Eaters of his "immortality" and instructed them to stay together if he appeared to die [Ch. 108]
- Dark Mark likely used for summoning and coordination
- Specific mechanics not detailed in text

**As Symbol**:
- Skull masks worn during operations
- Identified Death Eaters to victims and each other
- Symbol of terror during war

## Methods and Tactics [EXPLICIT Ch. 3]

### Terror and Intimidation

**Example: Yermy Wibble** [Ch. 3]:
- "Old and respected journalist" called for increased taxes and conscription
- "Shouted that it was absurd for the many to cower in fear of the few"
- His skin, wife's skin, and two daughters' skins "found nailed to the newsroom wall that next morning"
- Message: "Whoever stood out the most became the next example"

**Effect on Society**:
- "Everyone wished for something more to be done, and no-one dared take the lead to propose it"
- Other countries "wrung their hands but hesitated to intervene, whether out of apathetic selfishness or simple fear"
- "Whichever was first among them to oppose the Dark Lord, their peace would be the next target of his terror" [Ch. 3]

### Political Manipulation

- Used "wealth...and political power, and secrets held in blackmail, to paralyse a society trying to protect itself" [Ch. 3]
- Infiltrated Ministry and Wizengamot
- Post-defeat, maintained influence through legal means

## Defeat and Aftermath

### October 31, 1981 [Ch. 3, throughout]

**Godric's Hollow**:
- Voldemort attacked Potter family
- James Potter died defending family
- Lily Potter's sacrifice created protection
- Voldemort's Killing Curse rebounded, destroying his body
- Harry Potter survived with scar

**Immediate Aftermath**:
- Death Eaters scattered
- Some captured and imprisoned
- Others claimed Imperius defense
- Bellatrix attacked Longbottoms seeking information about Voldemort's fate

### Long-Term Consequences

**Political**:
- Blood purist faction retained power in Wizengamot
- Many former Death Eaters (or allies) still influential
- Dumbledore's faction gained some ground but not dominant

**Social**:
- Death Eater = career-ending accusation if proven
- But "reformed" Death Eaters could maintain respectability if wealthy/connected
- Ongoing tension between blood purists and reformers

**Azkaban Population**:
- Bellatrix and approximately dozen others imprisoned
- No trials in some cases (Sirius Black) [Ch. 29]
- Remained imprisoned until Bellatrix's rescue [Ch. 55-57]

## Key Ideology [Ch. 3, 108]

### Blood Purism

**Official Doctrine**:
- "Blood purism as argued by drunks in Knockturn Alley" [Ch. 108] - deliberate crude appeal
- Wizard supremacy over Muggles
- Pure-blood supremacy over Muggle-borns
- Maintenance of Noble House privilege

**Voldemort's True View**:
- Voldemort personally didn't believe blood purity ideology [IMPLIED Ch. 108]
- Used it as recruitment tool and political platform
- "Pointlessly cruel to his underlings" was calculated persona
- "Naked personal ambition" primary actual motive

### Methods

**Fear and Control**:
- Public displays of cruelty (Yermy Wibble example)
- Torture of opponents and their families
- Blackmail and political pressure
- Wealth and influence used for paralysis of opposition

## HPMOR vs Canon Differences

**Similarities to Canon**:
- Death Eaters as Voldemort's followers
- Skull masks and dark robes
- Bellatrix as most devoted follower
- Lucius Malfoy as politically connected member
- Defeat in 1981, some imprisoned in Azkaban
- Blood purist ideology

**HPMOR Specific Details**:
- **Origin as "practice identity"**: Voldemort admits Death Eaters started as disposable experiment [Ch. 108]
- **"Hired in a tavern"**: First Death Eaters literally recruited for pay, given costumes [Ch. 108]
- **Deliberate crudeness**: Blood purism "as argued by drunks" - intentionally unsophisticated appeal
- **Voldemort's disbelief**: He didn't actually believe the ideology, used it instrumentally
- **Bellatrix broken, not Imperiused**: Canon suggests Imperius/devotion; HPMOR explicit about "torture, and Legilimency, and rituals" [Ch. 51]
- **36 Death Eaters respond to final summons**: Specific number from hp-exam.tex
- **Obliviation of Death Eaters post-defeat**: Harry Obliviates them of final battle [Ch. 113-122]

**Tone Difference**:
- Canon Death Eaters: True believers in blood purity, fascistic movement
- HPMOR Death Eaters: Started as mercenaries in costumes, evolved into tool of Voldemort's ambition
- More emphasis on political/economic power ("wealth...political power...blackmail") than magical terror alone

## Implications for Fanfiction Writers

**What's Established**:
- Death Eaters began as literal hired mercenaries given costumes
- Voldemort didn't truly believe blood purity ideology
- 36 Death Eaters respond to Voldemort's final summons
- Bellatrix broken through torture/Legilimency/dark rituals (not Imperius)
- Lucius Malfoy avoided punishment, maintains political power
- Death Eaters used wealth, political power, and blackmail (not just magic)
- Post-final battle, Death Eaters Obliviated and believe Voldemort permanently dead

**What's Open to Interpretation**:
- Names of most Death Eaters besides Bellatrix, Lucius, and few others [AMBIGUOUS]
- Exact Dark Mark mechanics beyond summoning function [AMBIGUOUS]
- How many total Death Eaters existed during war [AMBIGUOUS - "36" at final summons is subset]
- Which specific Death Eaters avoided punishment via Imperius claims [AMBIGUOUS]
- What happened to Death Eaters who didn't respond to final summons [AMBIGUOUS]

**Common Mistakes to Avoid**:
- Don't portray Death Eaters as unified ideological movement - started as paid mercenaries
- Don't assume Voldemort believed blood purity - he explicitly created it as cynical political tool
- Don't forget Bellatrix's torture was deliberate "breaking," not Imperius Curse
- Don't assume Death Eaters were all magically powerful - they relied on "wealth...political power...blackmail"
- Don't ignore that Lucius specifically avoided punishment and remains politically powerful

**Useful for Stories**:
- Post-war Death Eater families navigating changed society
- Contrast between true believers and opportunists
- Political rehabilitation of former Death Eaters
- Bellatrix's psychological state after torture/breaking
- Death Eater children (Draco, etc.) dealing with parents' past
- Former Death Eaters maintaining blood purist agenda through legal means
- Obliviated Death Eaters' confused memories of war's end
