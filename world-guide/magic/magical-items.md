# Magical Items - Artifacts & Devices

## Overview

Magical items in HPMOR range from common enchanted objects to legendary artifacts of immense power. This document catalogs significant magical items, their properties, limitations, and significance. Many of the most powerful items are unique and irreplaceable, while others can be manufactured by skilled wizards.

---

## Deathly Hallows

### The Philosopher's Stone (Stone of Permanency)

**Type**: Legendary Artifact
**Creator**: Unknown (predates Nicholas Flamel by centuries)
**Current Owner**: Stolen by Voldemort [Ch. 108]

**Powers**:
- "The one and only power of the Stone is the imposition of permanency" [Ch. 108]
- Renders temporary forms into true and lasting substances [Ch. 108]
- Makes Transfigurations permanent [Ch. 108]
- Can reverse aging (parceled out carefully by Perenelle) [Ch. 108]

**Limitations**:
- Only one Stone exists [Ch. 108]
- "It is theorized that the Metamorphmagus curse merely rearranges the substance of their flesh" [Ch. 108]
- "Even Metamorphmagi cannot manifest golden fingernails and then trim them for sale" without the Stone [Ch. 108]
- Cannot be manufactured through alchemy—Nicholas Flamel's published recipe is a deliberate lie [Ch. 108]

**History**:
- "A very old thing indeed" - predates Flamel by far [Ch. 108]
- Originally possessed by Baba Yaga (Dark Lady) six centuries ago [Ch. 108]
- Stolen from Baba Yaga by Perenelle through seduction and betrayal [Ch. 108]
- Perenelle assumed the identity "Nicholas Flamel" and posed as Flamel's wife [Ch. 108]
- Used to extend lifespan and hold wizards hostage for secrets [Ch. 108]
- Perenelle took Unbreakable Vows not to be coerced into relinquishing the Stone [Ch. 108]

**Notable Quote**:
"If Merlin himself could create gold from thin air, history does not record it. So the Stone, we can guess even before research, must be a very old thing indeed." [Ch. 108]

### The Resurrection Stone

**Type**: Deathly Hallow
**Creator**: Cadmus Peverell (one of the three brothers)
**Current Status**: Integrated into Voldemort's Horcrux system [Ch. 108]

**Powers**:
- Does NOT actually bring back the dead [Ch. 108]
- "Holds a more ancient magic...for projecting the seeming of a spirit" [Ch. 108]
- Acknowledged Voldemort as its master (as one who has "defeated death") [Ch. 108]
- "Answered all my will" - extremely versatile for spirit projection [Ch. 108]

**Integration with Horcrux System**:
- "I have now incorporated it into my great creation" [Ch. 108]
- Freed Voldemort's spirit to "fly where it pleases and seduce the most opportune victim" [Ch. 108]
- Previously, Voldemort could only possess someone who contacted a physical horcrux
- Now can possess any victim who consents or is too weak to refuse
- "It is a catastrophe for any who oppose me" [Ch. 108]

**Original Location**:
- Hidden in Slytherin's ring (Voldemort's first traditional horcrux) [Ch. 108]
- Harry inadvertently revealed its location to Voldemort by drawing it on tea-saucer [Ch. 108]

**Notable Quote**:
"You brought that about, you freed my spirit to fly where it pleases and seduce the most opportune victim, by being too casual with your secrets." [Ch. 108]

### The Invisibility Cloak (True Cloak of Invisibility)

**Type**: Deathly Hallow
**Creator**: Unknown (possibly Death itself, or Ignotus Peverell)
**Current Owner**: Harry Potter (inherited from James Potter)

**Powers**:
- Perfect invisibility that doesn't fade with time
- Unlike normal invisibility cloaks, never wears out
- One of the three Deathly Hallows
- Provides concealment even from powerful magical detection (with limits)

**Limitations**:
- Can be detected by some magic (e.g., Marauder's Map shows people under it)
- Does not conceal sounds or smells
- Does not prevent magical detection of the wearer's presence entirely

**History**:
- Passed down through Potter family line
- James Potter's possession; given to Harry
- Part of the three Deathly Hallows legend

### The Elder Wand

**Type**: Deathly Hallow
**Creator**: Unknown (possibly Death itself, or Antioch Peverell)
**Current Status**: Referenced but not detailed in main narrative

**Powers**:
- Most powerful wand in existence
- Loyalty transfers through conquest/defeat
- Part of the three Deathly Hallows legend

**Note**: Less prominently featured in HPMOR than in canon Harry Potter

## Time Devices

### Time-Turner

**Type**: Regulated Magical Device
**Manufacturer**: Unknown (Ministry-controlled)
**Distribution**: Restricted to authorized users only

**Powers**:
- Allows user to travel backwards in time [Ch. 14]
- Creates stable time loops with enforced consistency [Ch. 14, 17]

**Limitations** [Ch. 14]:
- "Cannot be used to move more than six hours backwards" [Ch. 14]
- "Can't be used more than six times in any day" [Ch. 14]
- Restriction appears to be physical constraint of device
- Information consistency enforced—universe prevents paradoxes [Ch. 17]

**Physical Description**:
- Hourglass with protective shell [Ch. 14]
- Shell has restrictions engraved/embedded
- "Can be fragile" - McGonagall mentions "strange things happening if they're broken" [Ch. 14]
- Harry suggested adding protective shell to prevent breakage [Ch. 14]

**Special Properties**:
- Information from Azkaban's future cannot reach its past through Time-Turners [Ch. 61]
- Creates single timeline—no branching universes [Ch. 14]
- "DO NOT MESS WITH TIME" - critical warning [Ch. 17]

**Usage Rules**:
- "Wizards are advised to avoid being seen by their past selves" [Ch. 14]
- If crossing paths, "first version should step aside and close his eyes at a known time" [Ch. 14]
- Can cause psychological disturbance to see yourself [Ch. 14]

**Tampering Detection**:
- Dumbledore's detection charms found no tampering on Harry's Time-Turner [Ch. 61]
- Shell tampering theoretically possible but difficult [Ch. 61]

**Notable Quote**:
"Oh, you can't change time!" [Ch. 14]

## School Artifacts

### The Sorting Hat

**Type**: Ancient Artifact (Hogwarts Founder Era)
**Creator**: The Four Founders of Hogwarts
**Location**: Hogwarts School

**Powers**:
- Legilimency—reads minds of students [Ch. 12]
- Assigns students to Houses based on personality, values, and choices [Ch. 12]
- Can speak and reason independently
- Holds memories and wisdom from all four Founders
- "An ancient device...what you would use to destroy [it would be] a spell of cursed fire" [Ch. 45]

**Limitations**:
- Cannot be replicated—"no-one could duplicate any more" [Ch. 28]
- Can be destroyed by Fiendfyre [Ch. 45]
- Bound to Hogwarts and its sorting function

**Notable Interaction**:
- Harry negotiated with Sorting Hat [Ch. 12]
- Hat considered Slytherin for Harry, but Harry refused
- Placed in Ravenclaw after consideration

### The Marauder's Map

**Type**: Student-Created Artifact
**Creators**: The Marauders (James Potter, Sirius Black, Remus Lupin, Peter Pettigrew)
**Current Owners**: Fred and George Weasley

**Powers**:
- "Extraordinarily powerful artefact, capable of tracking every sentient being on the school grounds, in real time, by name" [Ch. 25]
- Shows people even under Invisibility Cloaks
- Updates continuously with people's movements
- Real-time tracking system

**Origins**:
- "Almost certainly created during the original raising of Hogwarts" [Ch. 25]
- Marauders "managed to steal part of the Hogwarts security system, something probably forged by Salazar Slytherin himself" [Ch. 25]
- "Twist it into a tool for student pranking" [Ch. 25]

**Technical Issues**:
- "Errors were starting to pop up" [Ch. 25]
- "Intermittent one fixed itself again. Other one's same as ever" [Ch. 25]
- Likely only Dumbledore could fix if truly broken [Ch. 25]

**Notable Quote**:
"If Godric Gryffindor had been around to see it, he would have approved." [Ch. 25]

### The Goblet of Fire

**Type**: Ancient Magical Device
**Original Location**: Hogwarts, later Beauxbatons
**Current Status**: Stolen by Voldemort [Ch. 108]

**Powers**:
- Enforces magical contracts and binding oaths
- "Witness[es] and punish[es] the transgressor" [Ch. 108]
- Used for Unbreakable Vows and binding agreements

**Historical Use**:
- Used to bind Baba Yaga and Hogwarts students/staff in oath [Ch. 108]
- "Some curses are easier to cast when they bind yourself and others alike" [Ch. 108]
- After Baba Yaga incident, "used to oversee pointless inter-school tournaments" [Ch. 108]
- Resided "in a disused chamber at Beauxbatons" until Voldemort stole it [Ch. 108]

**Baba Yaga Incident**:
- Students and Baba Yaga swore not to shed each other's blood or take each other's possessions
- Perenelle tricked Baba Yaga into being "forsworn" (virginity counted as "blood")
- Goblet rendered Baba Yaga defenseless as punishment
- "That was the end of the pact by which Dark Wizards and Witches taught Battle Magic at Hogwarts" [Ch. 108]

## Common Magical Objects

### Remembrall

**Type**: Memory Aid Device
**Appearance**: Glass sphere that glows red
**Function**: Glows red when you've forgotten something [Ch. 10]

**Limitations**:
- Doesn't tell you WHAT you've forgotten
- Only indicates that something was forgotten
- Relatively common item

**Notable Use**: Neville Longbottom owned one

### Broomsticks (Flying)

**Type**: Enchanted Transportation
**Variations**: Multiple quality levels and models

**Properties**:
- Operate on "Aristotelian physics, not Newtonian physics" [Ch. 59]
- One of the most dangerous discoveries about magic
- Flight seems to violate conventional physics understanding

**Limitations**:
- Cannot Transfigure a regular broomstick into a flying one [Ch. 28]
- Magical properties are separate from physical form
- Requires enchantment, not just physical structure

### Magical Trunks

**Type**: Enchanted Storage
**Features**: Expanded internal space [Ch. 6]

**Variations**:
- Multiple compartments
- Some with special properties (temperature control, etc.)
- Harry received one for Hogwarts

### Wands

**Type**: Magical Focus/Tool
**Importance**: Essential for most magic

**Properties**:
- Each wand is unique (wood, core, length, flexibility)
- "The wand chooses the wizard"
- Can show last spell cast via Prior Incantato [Ch. 13]

**Limitations**:
- Doesn't create magic—focuses wizard's innate power
- Required for most spells (some wandless magic possible)
- Can be disarmed/taken

## Potions and Consumables

### Comed-Tea

**Type**: Enchanted Beverage
**Creator**: Unknown

**Properties**:
- Causes backwards causation [Ch. 14]
- "The spell doesn't force funny events to happen, it just makes you feel an impulse to drink right before funny things are going to happen anyway!" [Ch. 14]
- "Drinking the Comed-Tea doesn't cause the comedy, the comedy causes you to drink the Comed-Tea!" [Ch. 14]
- "All makes sense once you draw the causal arrows going backwards in time!" [Ch. 14]

**Mechanism**:
- Effect precedes cause temporally
- Not prophetic—just time-reversed causation
- Relatively benign use of temporal magic

### Numbcloth

**Type**: Medical Supply
**Function**: Removes all feeling from applied area

**Limitations**:
- "Can only safely be used for up to thirty minutes" [Ch. 23]
- Comes off automatically after time limit
- "Not reusable for 24 hours" [Ch. 23]

**Notable Use**: Harry used it to numb hand after torture curse [Ch. 23]

### Felix Felicis (Liquid Luck)

**Type**: Potion
**Effect**: Provides luck for limited duration

**Limitations**:
- Referenced but not detailed in main narrative
- Presumably rare and difficult to brew
- Likely banned in competitions

## Manufacturing and Limitations

### What Can Be Made

1. **Common Enchanted Objects**:
   - Broomsticks (with proper skill)
   - Simple magical items
   - Potions

2. **Complex Artifacts**:
   - Require exceptional skill
   - Many ancient techniques lost due to Interdict of Merlin [Ch. 23, 25]
   - Some items (like Sorting Hat) cannot be duplicated [Ch. 28]

### What Cannot Be Made

1. **Philosopher's Stone**:
   - Only one exists [Ch. 108]
   - Alchemical recipe is a lie [Ch. 108]
   - Cannot be manufactured

2. **Deathly Hallows**:
   - Unique artifacts
   - Cannot be replicated
   - Legendary origins

3. **Founder-Era Artifacts**:
   - Many techniques lost [Ch. 23]
   - Interdict of Merlin prevents knowledge recovery [Ch. 23, 25]
   - Original creators' knowledge died with them

### Permanency Requirements

**Without Philosopher's Stone** [Ch. 108]:
- Transfigurations are temporary [Ch. 15, 28]
- "Conjurations such as the castle Hogwarts are maintained by a constant well of magic" [Ch. 108]
- Cannot permanently create matter or magical objects

**With Philosopher's Stone** [Ch. 108]:
- Can make Transfigurations permanent
- "The one and only power of the Stone is the imposition of permanency" [Ch. 108]
- Makes temporary forms into "true and lasting substance" [Ch. 108]

## Implications for Fanfiction Writers

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Don't trivialize unique artifacts**:
   - There's only ONE Philosopher's Stone
   - Deathly Hallows cannot be replicated
   - Not every powerful wizard has legendary artifacts

2. **Don't forget manufacturing limitations**:
   - Most powerful artifacts can't be made anymore
   - Interdict of Merlin ensures techniques are lost [Ch. 23, 25]
   - Even Transfiguration requires the Stone for permanence

3. **Don't ignore Time-Turner rules**:
   - Six-hour limit is absolute [Ch. 14]
   - Creates single timeline, not branches [Ch. 14]
   - Universe enforces consistency [Ch. 17]

4. **Don't make magical items too convenient**:
   - Items have real limitations
   - Costs and tradeoffs matter
   - Powerful items are rare for good reasons

### What You Can Do

1. **Explore lost artifacts**:
   - Many powerful items exist in ruins or lost locations
   - Characters can quest for ancient artifacts
   - Discovery doesn't mean understanding how to use

2. **Create reasonable new items**:
   - Extrapolate from existing magical principles
   - Consider manufacturing difficulty
   - Include meaningful limitations

3. **Use items to create problems, not just solve them**:
   - Time-Turner creates as many complications as solutions
   - Philosopher's Stone is source of conflict, not easy answer
   - Powerful artifacts attract dangerous attention

4. **Respect historical significance**:
   - Founder-era items are irreplaceable
   - Loss of manufacturing knowledge is real problem
   - Characters should treat artifacts with appropriate gravity

### What's Open to Interpretation

1. **Minor magical items**:
   - Exactly what can be enchanted and how
   - Limits of common magical manufacturing
   - Cost and difficulty of various enchantments

2. **Artifact interactions**:
   - How different powerful items interact
   - Whether combinations create new effects
   - Synergies or conflicts between artifacts

3. **Undiscovered items**:
   - What else might exist from Founder era
   - Items from other magical civilizations
   - Atlantean artifacts

4. **Modification possibilities**:
   - Can existing items be enhanced?
   - What happens if you break/modify an artifact?
   - Are there safe ways to study dangerous items?
