# Patronus Charm - Patronus 2.0

## Overview

The Patronus Charm (incantation: "Expecto Patronum") creates a guardian of positive emotion that shields against Dementors and other dark creatures. In HPMOR, Harry discovers a fundamentally different version - the **True Patronus** [Ch. 45] - powered not by happy memories but by the absolute rejection of Death itself, creating a corporeal human form that can actually destroy Dementors rather than merely repelling them.

## Established Rules [EXPLICIT]

### Traditional Patronus Charm

1. **Requires Happy Thoughts and Complex Wandwork** [Ch. 43]
   - "Early gestures of the spell were complex and precise; you twitched your wand once, twice, thrice, and four times with small tilts at exactly the right relative angles" [Ch. 43]
   - Requires "warm, happy feelings that you kept close in your heart, the loving memories" [Ch. 43]
   - Not difficult because it needs much magic, but because "it took more than mere magic" [Ch. 43]
   - Different kind of strength than ordinary spells require

2. **Ministry Claims It Requires Fifth Year** [Ch. 43]
   - Ministry believes it's futile to teach before fifth year
   - "A few known cases of younger children learning it" dismissed as "genius" [Ch. 43]
   - Professor Quirrell proves this wrong - children can learn it if taught properly [Ch. 43]
   - Quote: "Ministry Committee of Curriculum would have been of greater benefit to wizardkind if they had been used as landfill" [Ch. 43]

3. **Why Children Learn It Better** [Ch. 43]
   - Professor Quirrell: "A Dementor attacks through fear, and children are less afraid" [Ch. 43]
   - Dumbledore initially disagrees, saying "adults had more courage"
   - Quirrell's phrasing: "Adults had more courage, not less to fear" [Ch. 43]
   - Children have less life experience, fewer traumatic memories, less awareness of mortality

4. **Three Levels of Success** [Ch. 43]
   - **No effect**: Total failure, wand doesn't respond
   - **Shielding mist**: Sparkling white mist that provides some protection
   - **Corporeal Patronus**: Takes animal form, provides complete protection, very rare and powerful

5. **Animal Forms Have Meaning** [IMPLIED] [Ch. 43]
   - Each wizard's corporeal Patronus takes a specific animal shape
   - "The animals didn't know, and so were sheltered from the fear" [Ch. 45]
   - Animal forms protect the caster by *not understanding* what Dementors truly are
   - Animals can be: peregrine falcon, otter, bear, panther, orang-utan, phoenix (Dumbledore), etc.

### Dementor Training Rules

6. **Real Dementors May Help** [Ch. 43]
   - "Often, wizards who couldn't cast the Patronus Charm in practice would be able to do so in the presence of a real Dementor" [Ch. 43]
   - Can go "from flat failure all the way to a full corporeal Patronus" [Ch. 43]
   - "Defied all logic, the Dementor's aura of fear ought to make it harder" [Ch. 43]
   - "But that was the way it was" [Ch. 43]

7. **Training Exposure Limits** [Ch. 43]
   - "Up to twenty seconds of exposure at five paces was certainly safe" for eleven-year-olds [Ch. 43]
   - "Forty seconds of Dementor exposure at five paces might possibly have been enough to cause permanent damage" [Ch. 43]
   - "Not every hurt could be healed afterwards" [Ch. 43]
   - Headmaster maintains Patronus, then dispels it for testing period

### Who Cannot Cast It

8. **Good People Who Aren't Happy Cannot Cast Patronus** [EXPLICIT] [Ch. 43]
   - **Godric Gryffindor** never cast a Patronus despite being a hero [Ch. 43]
   - "Godric Gryffindor had been a good man, not a happy one" [Ch. 43]
   - Fought Dark Lords, protected commoners, lost half his friends in good causes
   - Listened to screams of wounded in his armies, buried young wizards who rallied to him
   - On deathbed told Helga that red and gold should be "bright warm colours" - students should be happier than him [Ch. 43]

9. **Rowena Ravenclaw Also Couldn't Cast It** [IMPLIED] [Ch. 43]
   - Hermione seems to think of her when learning she can't cast it
   - Both were intellectuals who loved books
   - Implication: Deep thinkers may struggle with the necessary happy thoughtlessness

10. **The Social Stigma** [Ch. 43]
    - "The rest of the school just seemed to think that No Patronus meant Bad Person, plain and simple" [Ch. 43]
    - People assumed inability to cast = Dark Wizard
    - "Maybe the other failures didn't mention it because it was less shameful...to be thought Dark rather than unhappy" [Ch. 43]
    - Hermione's fans felt she'd "failed them" when she couldn't cast it [Ch. 43]

## Harry's Discovery: The True Patronus [EXPLICIT] [Ch. 45]

### The Riddle of the Dementor

11. **What Dementors Really Are** [Ch. 45]
    - Traditional answer to "What lies at the centre of a Dementor?": Fear
    - True answer: **Death itself**
    - "They are wounds in the world" - Godric Gryffindor [Ch. 45]
    - "Through some law of magic you are a shadow that Death casts into the world" [Ch. 45]
    - The fear is a *side effect*, not the main problem

12. **Why Most Wizards Get It Wrong** [Ch. 45]
    - People *understand* fear, know what to do about fear
    - Realizing fear is just a side effect is not comforting
    - "It wouldn't exactly be comforting to ask: 'What if the fear is just a side effect rather than the main problem?'" [Ch. 45]
    - Easier to believe the comfortable lie than face the deeper truth

### Requirements for the True Patronus

13. **You Must Reject Death** [EXPLICIT] [Ch. 45]
    - Traditional Patronus: Happy thoughts that ignore Death
    - True Patronus: "Absolute rejection of death as the natural order" [Ch. 45, 55]
    - Must think about "the value of human life" [Ch. 55]
    - Cannot look away from Death, must face it directly and defy it

14. **The Correct Mental State** [Ch. 45]
    - Harry used: "Everything that made life worth living" [Ch. 45]
    - Books his father bought, Mum's smile, parents' sacrifice
    - Hermione's friendship, Draco's journey to the light
    - All the students he would protect
    - The Earth amid the stars, humanity's future among the galaxy
    - The promise that Death will someday be defeated

15. **The Critical Thought** [Ch. 45]
    - "And Death is not something I will ever embrace" [Ch. 45]
    - "It is only a childish thing, that the human species has not yet outgrown" [Ch. 45]
    - "And some day... We'll get over it... And people won't have to say goodbye any more" [Ch. 45]
    - Not happiness *despite* Death, but defiance *of* Death

### The True Patronus Form

16. **Takes Human Shape** [EXPLICIT] [Ch. 45]
    - "A figure with two arms, two legs, and a head, standing upright" [Ch. 45]
    - "The animal Homo sapiens, the shape of a human being" [Ch. 45]
    - Not an animal form - animals don't understand Death, can't truly reject it
    - Represents humanity's unique capacity to fight Death through intelligence

17. **Brightness Beyond Normal** [Ch. 45]
    - "Glowing brighter and brighter...blazing with incandescent light brighter than the fading sunset" [Ch. 45]
    - "Shone more brilliant now than the noonday Sun, so radiant that Harry could feel the warmth of it on his skin" [Ch. 45]
    - Far brighter than any traditional corporeal Patronus
    - Aurors had to shield their eyes from it

18. **Can DESTROY Dementors** [EXPLICIT] [Ch. 45]
    - Traditional Patronus: Repels Dementors
    - True Patronus: "The tattered cloak lay empty within the cage" [Ch. 45]
    - Harry actually destroyed the Dementor completely
    - This was thought impossible - "Dementors cannot be killed. Many have tried" [Ch. 45]
    - Even cursed fire (Fiendfyre) "has no effect on Dementors" [Ch. 45]

### Why It Works

19. **The Deeper Truth** [INFERRED] [Ch. 45]
    - Dementors are "wounds in the world" - manifestations of Death
    - "Attacking a wound only makes it larger" [Ch. 45]
    - Traditional spells attack directly, trying to destroy the Dementor as an enemy
    - True Patronus doesn't attack Death - it *affirms Life*
    - The affirmation of life's value heals the wound, closes it
    - Quote: "Until the wound in the world is healed at last" [Ch. 45]

## Casting Under Dementor Influence [EXPLICIT] [Ch. 55]

### The Danger of Losing Your Thought

20. **Dementors Interfere With Memory** [Ch. 55]
    - Harry in Azkaban: "He couldn't...quite remember...what he'd used as his happy thought" [Ch. 55]
    - "The presence of Dementors interferes with the part of you that processes happiness" [Ch. 55]
    - Even the True Patronus thought can be forgotten under Dementation

21. **Recovery Through Logic** [Ch. 55]
    - Harry's technique: Treat Dementor's influence as cognitive bias
    - "Think of the Dementors' fear as a cognitive bias, and try to overcome it the way you would overcome any other cognitive bias" [Ch. 55]
    - "All negative emotions and pessimistic estimates must now be considered suspect, fallacious until proven valid" [Ch. 55]
    - Retrieve memory through logical reconstruction rather than emotional association

22. **Alternative Access Routes** [Ch. 55]
    - If you can't remember via happiness, try other associations
    - Harry remembered: When did I last talk about Patronus? About Dementors?
    - Remembered stars (taught by Quirrell), remembered his words about rejection of death
    - "There were words he had spoken out loud...his mind could remember the sounds and the speech even as the feelings seemed distant" [Ch. 55]

23. **The Restored Patronus** [Ch. 55]
    - "The words came out a little halting, and when the human shape burst back into existence it was dim at first" [Ch. 55]
    - "Moonlight instead of sunlight, white instead of silver" [Ch. 55]
    - "But it strengthened, slowly, as Harry breathed in deliberate rhythm, recovering" [Ch. 55]
    - Can be recast even under severe Dementation through discipline and technique

## Effects of True Patronus [EXPLICIT]

### On Dementors

24. **Instant Destruction** [Ch. 45]
    - One True Patronus completely destroyed one Dementor
    - No remains, just empty cloak
    - Effect appeared immediate and total

### On Recently Demented People

25. **Can Restore Recent Losses** [INFERRED] [Ch. 55]
    - Bellatrix: "Oh, I feel a little better now, how strange" after Harry's Patronus blazed [Ch. 55]
    - Harry's theory: "What the Dementors had taken from you recently, the True Patronus Charm might give back" [Ch. 55]
    - "Like the difference between emptying a cup, and the unused cup fading away" [Ch. 55]
    - Bellatrix regained strength/magic lost in last week, but not happy memories eaten years ago
    - "Like the equivalent of getting a week of rest, a week to build up her magic again" [Ch. 55]

## Known Limitations [EXPLICIT]

### What Traditional Patronus Cannot Do

1. **Cannot Kill Dementors** [Ch. 45]
   - Can only repel them temporarily
   - Dementors considered "undying" and "invincible" [Ch. 45]
   - Even powerful corporeal Patronuses only drive them back

2. **Relies on Not Understanding** [Ch. 45]
   - Animal forms work because animals don't comprehend Death
   - If you truly understand what Dementors are, traditional Patronus won't work
   - Harry and Hermione both failed traditional Patronus because they understood too much
   - "It felt wrong" when trying to cast [Ch. 43]

3. **Requires Ability to Be Happy** [Ch. 43]
   - If you cannot access happy memories, you cannot cast it
   - Traumatized wizards struggle or fail
   - Good people who've suffered too much may be unable to cast

### What True Patronus Cannot Do

1. **Cannot Be Cast Without Understanding Death** [IMPLIED]
   - Must truly comprehend what Death is
   - Must face it without flinching
   - Most wizards refuse to look that deeply
   - Requires "courage to face it" [Ch. 45]

2. **Cannot Be Taught Easily** [IMPLIED]
   - Harry couldn't fully explain it to others (mentioned but not shown)
   - Requires philosophical understanding beyond most wizards
   - Must genuinely believe Death is wrong and defeatable
   - Cannot fake the rejection of Death

## Teaching Others [IMPLIED] [Ch. 55-56]

### Challenges

- Harry taught Draco and others in army some form of improved Patronus [Ch. 55-56 reference]
- Exact methodology not fully detailed
- Likely taught "think about the value of human life" rather than full Death-rejection
- Question: Can partial understanding yield partial results?

## Edge Cases & Ambiguities [AMBIGUOUS]

### Unresolved Questions

1. **Can others learn the True Patronus?**
   - Harry discovered it, but can it be taught?
   - Does it require his specific philosophical understanding?
   - Could Hermione learn it if taught properly?

2. **What happens to destroyed Dementors?**
   - Do they reform elsewhere?
   - Are they permanently gone?
   - Does destroying them "heal" something in the world?

3. **Why does thinking of life destroy Death's shadow?**
   - The mechanism is philosophical, not explained
   - "Wounds in the world" metaphor suggests ontological status
   - How does affirming life heal the wound?

4. **Can True Patronus restore deeply Demented people?**
   - Bellatrix recovered recent losses
   - Could it restore someone Demented for years?
   - What about victims of Dementor's Kiss?

5. **Is the human form always correct for True Patronus?**
   - Harry's is human because humanity will defeat Death
   - Would alien intelligence have different form?
   - Is it species-specific or concept-specific?

6. **How many people could cast True Patronus?**
   - Requires rejecting Death as natural
   - Requires belief that Death can be defeated
   - Most wizards accept Death as inevitable
   - Very rare mindset even among rationalists

## Implications for Fanfiction Writers

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Don't trivialize the True Patronus**
   - It required Harry's entire philosophical worldview
   - Not just "think happy thoughts about defeating death"
   - Requires genuine, bone-deep belief that Death is wrong
   - Most people, even good people, don't have this belief

2. **Don't forget the traditional Patronus costs**
   - Goodness â‰  ability to cast Patronus
   - Trauma, depression, deep thought all interfere
   - It's not a "good person detector"
   - Godric Gryffindor couldn't cast it!

3. **Don't ignore the animal form meaning**
   - Animals serve a purpose - they don't understand Death
   - If your character deeply understands mortality, animal form may not work
   - Human form is specific to rejecting Death itself

4. **Don't make Patronus teaching easy**
   - Ministry's fifth-year requirement isn't arbitrary stupidity
   - It genuinely requires emotional and magical maturity
   - Quirrell proved children *can* learn with proper teaching, but it's still hard

### What You Can Do

1. **Explore the philosophy**
   - Different characters' relationships with Death
   - Why some can/can't cast Patronus
   - What it means to truly reject Death vs. merely fearing it

2. **Vary Patronus forms meaningfully**
   - Animal choice reflects something about the caster
   - Characters might have different forms at different life stages
   - True Patronus (human) vs. traditional (animal) has deep significance

3. **Use Patronus as character development**
   - Learning to cast it shows growth
   - Losing ability shows trauma
   - Form changing shows fundamental change in person

4. **Play with the "wound in the world" concept**
   - What does it mean for Dementors to be wounds?
   - Are there other wounds? Other shadows Death casts?
   - Can True Patronus heal other things?

### What's Open to Interpretation

1. **Exact requirements for True Patronus**
   - Harry's version is clear, but could there be variants?
   - Different philosophical frameworks that also work?
   - Cultural variations in how Death is rejected?

2. **Whether Dementors are truly gone when destroyed**
   - Canon Harry Potter says they can't be killed
   - HPMOR Harry proved otherwise
   - But are they destroyed or just... dispersed? Reformed elsewhere?

3. **Teaching methodology**
   - How much can be taught vs. must be discovered?
   - Can you teach someone to reject Death?
   - What's the minimum understanding needed?

4. **Other applications**
   - If True Patronus heals wounds in the world, what else can it do?
   - Could it work against other dark magic?
   - Does it have positive effects beyond anti-Dementor use?
