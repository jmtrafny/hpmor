# Minerva McGonagall

## Basic Information

- **Full Name**: Minerva McGonagall
- **House**: Gryffindor (as student)
- **Role**: Deputy Headmistress; Transfiguration Professor; Head of Gryffindor; competent voice of reason
- **First Appearance**: Ch. 1
- **Chapter Count**: Appears in approximately 40 chapters

## HPMOR vs Canon Divergences

**CRITICAL DIFFERENCES:**

1. **More Competent**: Less blindly loyal to Dumbledore, more independently thinking.

2. **Frustrated by Dumbledore**: Sees his manipulation, disagrees with methods, but ultimately defers.

3. **First Contact with Harry**: Ch. 1 - the "destroying the world" conversation establishes their relationship.

4. **Transfiguration Mastery**: Her teaching reveals depth of magic through Harry's innovations.

5. **Professional**: Competent administrator, not just Dumbledore's deputy.

6. **More Agency**: Makes own decisions, though often overruled by Headmaster.

7. **Eventually Goes Off-Script**: Post-Hermione's death, begins questioning role and conventions.

## Personality & Motivations

### Core Traits [EXPLICIT]

- **Competent and organized**: Runs Hogwarts efficiently as Deputy. Professional. [Throughout]

- **Scottish accent**: "Mr. Potter" with Scottish burr. Proper pronunciation. [Throughout]

- **Exasperated by Harry**: Recognizes brilliance but frustrated by rule-breaking and chaos. [Throughout]

- **Transfiguration master**: Deep understanding of magic shown through teaching. [Ch. 14+]

- **Loyal to Dumbledore**: But increasingly questioning. Shows strain. [Throughout]

- **"Going off-script"**: Post-tragedy, begins acting outside conventional role. [Ch. 90+]

## Writer Notes

### Common Mistakes

1. **Too rigid**: She's competent, not inflexible automaton
2. **Canon personality exactly**: More frustrated, more independent thinking here
3. **Missing Scottish accent**: Should be present in all dialogue
4. **Forgetting "off-script" development**: Post-tragedy, she changes

### Key Nuances

- Respects Harry's intelligence while frustrated by his chaos
- Loyal to Dumbledore but sees his flaws
- "Going off-script" is major character development
- Professional but capable of growth and change

### Writing Successfully

- Scottish accent in all dialogue
- Professional formality
- Exasperation with Harry should be fond underneath
- Show competence in Transfiguration
- Post-tragedy: show her questioning her role
