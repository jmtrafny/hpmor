# Severus Snape

## Basic Information

- **Full Name**: Severus Snape
- **House**: Slytherin (as student)
- **Role**: Potions Master; Head of Slytherin; bitter guardian; eventually departing
- **First Appearance**: Ch. 9
- **Chapter Count**: Appears in approximately 25 chapters (especially Ch. 27, 76-78, 121)

## HPMOR vs Canon Divergences

**CRITICAL DIFFERENCES:**

1. **More Competent**: Genuinely skilled at magic and potions, less petty than canon version.

2. **Still Bitter**: But bitterness is more constrained, controlled. Understands professionalism.

3. **Departure**: Eventually leaves Hogwarts (Ch. 121), not killed. Gets to move on with life.

4. **Relationship with Harry**: Still antagonistic but Harry blackmails Dumbledore to stop worst bullying (Ch. 18).

5. **Lily Backstory**: Still loves Lily, still regrets, but more functional about it.

6. **Less Cartoonishly Mean**: Cruel but within bounds of harsh professor, not abusive monster.

7. **Ultimate Liberation**: Gets to leave, find new life, not die tragically.

## Personality & Motivations

### Core Traits [EXPLICIT]

- **Bitter and sarcastic**: "Potter" said with contempt. Cutting remarks. [Throughout]

- **Competent Potions Master**: Actually skilled, teaches well when not letting bitterness interfere. [Ch. 27]

- **Trapped by past**: Can't move beyond Lily's death, James's shadow, his own choices. [Throughout]

- **Protective (reluctantly)**: Watches over Harry because of Lily, resents it. [Throughout]

- **Professional**: Unlike canon, maintains standards. Not randomly abusive. [Throughout]

### Voice & Speech Patterns

**Vocabulary**: Precise, acidic, formal

**Common Phrases**:
- "Potter" (with contempt, not "Mr. Potter" or "Harry")
- Sarcastic observations
- Cutting put-downs
- Formal classroom language

**Emotional Range**:
- Default: Cold, controlled contempt
- Angry: Icy, dangerous quiet
- Rare vulnerability: About Lily

### Dialogue Samples

**Contemptuous:**
> "Potter..." [Said with dripping contempt] [Throughout]

**Sarcastic:**
> [Cutting remarks about student incompetence] [Throughout]

**Professional:**
> [Teaching actual potions when not being bitter] [Ch. 27]

## Writer Notes

### Common Mistakes

1. **Too sympathetic**: Still bitter, still cruel within bounds
2. **Canon abuse levels**: More controlled here, less petty
3. **Missing competence**: Actually skilled teacher when professional
4. **Forgetting eventual liberation**: Gets to leave, start over

### Key Nuances

- Trapped between duty to Lily and resentment of son
- Competent but bitterness limits effectiveness
- Eventually gets freedom (Ch. 121)
- Not irredeemable but not easily redeemed either

### Writing Successfully

- Controlled contempt in voice
- Professional cruelty, not random meanness
- Show competence in magic
- Remember he eventually escapes this role
