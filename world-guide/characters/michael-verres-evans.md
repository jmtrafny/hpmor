# Michael Verres-Evans

## Basic Information

- **Full Name**: Michael Verres-Evans (took wife's name)
- **House**: N/A (Muggle)
- **Role**: Harry's adoptive father; Oxford professor; rationalist educator; loving but limited parent
- **First Appearance**: Ch. 1
- **Chapter Count**: Appears in early chapters (1-6), mentioned later, appears briefly Ch. 36

## HPMOR vs Canon Divergences

**CRITICAL DIFFERENCES:**

1. **Scientist/Academic**: Oxford biochemistry professor. Intelligent, educated. Opposite of Vernon Dursley.

2. **Taught Harry Science**: Raised him with Feynman Lectures, scientific method, rationality from age 5.

3. **Loving Parent**: Genuinely cares for Harry, supportive, invested in his education.

4. **Took Wife's Name**: "Verres-Evans" because Petunia was more valuable (beautiful, magical transformation).

5. **Can't Perceive Magic**: Has difficulty even looking at magical objects directly. Squib-adjacent?

6. **Supportive but Skeptical**: Encourages Harry but doesn't take him fully seriously as equal.

7. **Good Father**: Spent fortune on books for Harry, supports education, protective.

## Writer Notes

### Common Mistakes

1. **Making him bad father**: He's loving, supportive, invested
2. **Too much respect for Harry**: Doesn't quite take him as equal despite intelligence
3. **Forgetting academic profession**: He's Oxford professor, speaks/acts accordingly
4. **Missing magic limitation**: Can't perceive/interact with magic easily

### Key Nuances

- Taught Harry rationality but doesn't follow through to respecting Harry's conclusions
- Loving in his way - actions (buying books) more than words
- Cannot fully enter Harry's magical world (physical limitation)
- "Good Parent" role vs. actually listening to child
- Took wife's name - significant status flip

### Writing Successfully

- Academic voice, professorial
- Show love through actions (providing books, education)
- Respectful distance from magic
- Parental but not patronizing exactly - just limited
- Scientific language naturally integrated
