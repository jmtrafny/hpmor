# Lucius Malfoy

## Basic Information

- **Full Name**: Lucius Malfoy
- **House**: Slytherin (as student)
- **Role**: Draco's father; Wizengamot power player; political manipulator; patriarch of Noble House Malfoy
- **First Appearance**: Ch. 4 (at Diagon Alley), Ch. 79+ (major role)
- **Chapter Count**: Appears in approximately 15 chapters (especially Ch. 79-84)

## HPMOR vs Canon Divergences

**CRITICAL DIFFERENCES:**

1. **Actually Competent**: Skilled politician and schemer, not bumbling villain. Real threat.

2. **Political Animal**: Understands power, knows how to use Wizengamot, manipulates skillfully.

3. **Loves Draco**: Genuine care for son, not just using him. Parenting matters to him.

4. **Lost Status**: Post-Voldemort, lost power and influence. Trying to regain it.

5. **Aristocratic**: True old-family nobility. Knows how power works in magical Britain.

6. **Temporarily Allies with Harry**: Forms alliance to expose Dumbledore (Ch. 79-84).

7. **Not Purely Evil**: Ambitious, ruthless, but has values (family, House honor).

## Writer Notes

### Common Mistakes

1. **Making him stupid**: He's competent, skilled, dangerous
2. **Purely evil**: Has values, loves son, has honor (of a sort)
3. **Canon bumbling**: This Lucius is effective politician
4. **Missing political skill**: He's expert at soft power

### Key Nuances

- Genuinely loves Draco but parenting influenced by aristocratic values
- Lost power post-Voldemort, working to regain it
- Skilled politician, understands how power works
- Aristocratic honor matters (different from conventional morality)

### Writing Successfully

- Aristocratic formality in speech
- Show political skill, not just ambition
- Care for Draco should be evident
- Politically careful in public, more direct in private
