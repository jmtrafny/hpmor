# Hermione Granger

## Basic Information

- **Full Name**: Hermione Jean Granger
- **House**: Ravenclaw
- **Role**: Harry's best friend; General Sunshine; S.P.H.E.W. founder; ultimate moral anchor
- **First Appearance**: Ch. 9
- **Chapter Count**: Appears in approximately 70 chapters (heavy in early chapters, 64-90, 120-122)

## HPMOR vs Canon Divergences

**CRITICAL DIFFERENCES FROM CANON HERMIONE:**

1. **House**: Ravenclaw, not Gryffindor. More intellectual focus, surrounded by fellow scholars.

2. **Proactive Leadership**: Founds S.P.H.E.W. (Society for the Promotion of Heroic Equality for Witches), leads army as General Sunshine. Canon Hermione was more supportive than leading.

3. **Relationship with Harry**: Best friends/intellectual equals with romantic undertones. Both recognize the other's brilliance. Canon trio dynamic absent.

4. **Social Awareness**: Better social skills and emotional intelligence than HPMOR Harry. Can navigate complex situations.

5. **Tragic Arc**: Dies in Ch. 89 (troll attack), resurrected in Ch. 122. Transforms into "something like a unicorn" - innocence personified.

6. **General Role**: Leads army in battle, commands respect, not just "brightest witch." More heroic, proactive.

7. **Death and Resurrection**: Central tragedy of story. Her death drives Harry's character development and quest against death itself.

- **Brilliant and studious**: Knows 100 digits of pi "because that was how many digits had been printed in the back of her maths textbook." Learns spells instantly. [Ch. 9]

## Character Arc

### Part 1-2: The Brightest Witch (Ch 1-37)

**State**: Brilliant first-year, eager to prove herself, friendly academic rival to Harry.

**Key moments**:
- Ch. 9: Meeting Harry; recognizing fellow intellectual
- Ch. 15: Establishing study habits and excellence
- Ch. 36: Harry's defense of her to parents: "brightest star of Hogwarts"

### Part 3: Heroism and Sacrifice (Ch 38-63)

**State**: Becoming General Sunshine, leader, hero.

**Key moments**:
- Ch. 44: Kisses Harry to save him from Dementor/dark side
- Ch. 63: Fawkes considers her as potential bearer (rejects because she's TOO heroic)
- Founded S.P.H.E.W., actively fighting bullying

### Part 4: Tragedy (Ch 64-90)

**State**: Falsely accused, saved by Harry, then killed by troll.

**Key moments**:
- Ch. 78-79: Arrested for attempted murder of Draco (false accusation)
- Ch. 82-84: Harry spends Potter fortune to save her from Azkaban
- Ch. 89: Dies saving others from troll; last words "Not your fault"

### Part 5-6: Death and Return (Ch 91-122)

**State**: Dead, then resurrected with unicorn-like properties.

**Key moments**:
- Ch. 90-96: Harry refuses to accept death, preserves body
- Ch. 122: Resurrected; sworn friendship with Harry; established as head of Noble House Granger

## Voice & Speech Patterns

### Vocabulary & Complexity

- **Vocabulary Level**: Advanced but not technical like Harry. Academic, precise, earnest.
- **Sentence Structure**: Clear, organized, sometimes indignant when facing injustice.
- **Formality**: Polite, proper, follows social conventions more than Harry does.

### Characteristic Patterns

- **Common Phrases**:
  - "That's horrible!" (when confronting injustice)
  - "You shouldn't do that!" (moral remonstration)
  - "The book said..." (appeal to authority/learning)
  - Direct moral statements without qualification

- **What They Reference**: Books, lessons, proper procedure, rules

- **Emotional Range**:
  - Earnest and enthusiastic when learning
  - Indignant when facing wrong
  - Determined when leading
  - Vulnerable when uncertain about worth

## Writer Notes

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Making her just "smart girl"**: She's a leader, general, hero in her own right
2. **Forgetting moral core**: Her goodness is fundamental, not performance
3. **Missing emotional intelligence**: She understands people better than Harry
4. **Canon personality**: This is Ravenclaw Hermione, more proactive and heroic

### Key Nuances

1. **Death and resurrection change her**: Post-Ch. 122 Hermione has died, remembers it
2. **Unicorn-like properties**: Innocence made manifest, protected by Hogwarts wards
3. **Would sacrifice self for others**: Core character trait, led to death
4. **Harry's emotional anchor**: She keeps him human, connected to goodness

### Writing This Character Successfully

- Earnest voice without irony
- Moral clarity without self-righteousness
- Intelligence shown through action
- Heroism through sacrifice, not violence
- Balance of strength and vulnerability
