# Post-Story State (After June 1992)

## Overview

HPMOR concludes in June 1992 at the end of Harry's first year. This file documents the state of the wizarding world, character fates, and unresolved plot threads as of the story's conclusion (Chapters 120-122).

## Major Character Fates

### Harry James Potter-Evans-Verres [Ch. 120-122]

**Status**: Alive, Boy-Who-Lived, returning for Year Two

**Achievements**:
- Defeated Lord Voldemort permanently [Ch. 113]
- Rescued Hermione from death via resurrection [Ch. 119]
- Founded conspiracy with Hermione and Draco [Ch. 98]
- Discovered True Patronus Charm [Ch. 45-46]
- Killed Dementor, proving they can be destroyed [Ch. 45]

**Future Plans**:
- "Tear apart the Dementors" - destroy all Dementors [Ch. 121]
- Reform wizarding Britain's government and society
- Pursue immortality research for all humanity
- Continue education at Hogwarts
- Maintain secrecy about Voldemort's defeat [Ch. 113]

**Secrets Kept**:
- Harry defeated Voldemort (public believes "mysterious attacker")
- Hermione's resurrection method (Philosopher's Stone + Transfiguration)
- Quirrell was Voldemort
- True nature of many events during Year One

### Hermione Jean Granger [Ch. 119-122]

**Status**: Resurrected, Founder of House Granger, first Noble House founded by Muggle-born

**Transformation**:
- Died April 1992, killed by troll [Ch. 89]
- Resurrected via Philosopher's Stone and Transfiguration magic [Ch. 119]
- Became first unicorn Animagus (unprecedented) [Ch. 119]
- Body transformed during resurrection - now partly magical creature

**House Granger**: [Ch. 122]
- First Muggle-born to found Noble and Most Ancient House
- Challenges traditional blood purity hierarchy
- Independent from House Potter (initially debt-ward, now separate)
- Hermione as Lady Granger with full Noble House rights

**Future Plans**:
- Continue Hogwarts education
- Leverage Noble House status for reform
- Work with Harry and Draco on conspiracy
- Explore implications of unicorn Animagus form
- Challenge blood purity ideology through her existence

**Secrets Kept**:
- Details of resurrection method
- True nature of death and resurrection
- Extent of transformation from resurrection

### Draco Lucius Malfoy [Ch. 120-122]

**Status**: Alive, heir to House Malfoy, transformed by year's experiences

**Transformation**:
- Abandoned blood purity ideology [Ch. 23]
- Learned scientific method from Harry [Ch. 22-23]
- Nearly killed by Blood-Cooling Charm [Ch. 79]
- Witnessed Hermione's trial and Harry's sacrifice [Ch. 80-83]
- Reformed from blood purist to rational thinker

**Future Path**:
- Will inherit House Malfoy
- Part of Harry-Hermione-Draco conspiracy [Ch. 98]
- Plans to reform Slytherin House and pure-blood society from within
- Continue friendship/alliance with Harry despite political complications

**Family Complications**:
- Father (Lucius) still blood purist politician
- Draco must navigate loyalty to father vs. new beliefs
- Eventually will lead House Malfoy in different direction

### Albus Dumbledore [Ch. 120-122]

**Status**: Alive, remains Chief Warlock, uncertain future at Hogwarts

**Political Position**:
- Still Chief Warlock of Wizengamot [Ch. 80]
- Still Supreme Mugwump (international position)
- May or may not remain Headmaster [AMBIGUOUS]

**Relationship with Harry**:
- Strained after Year One events
- Knows some of Harry's secrets, not all
- Wary of Harry's power and plans
- Unclear future collaboration or conflict

### Severus Snape [Ch. 121]

**Status**: Departed Hogwarts

**Departure**: [Ch. 121]
- Left position as Potions Master
- Left position as Head of Slytherin
- Reasons for departure not fully explained
- May relate to Voldemort's final defeat and role in conspiracy

**Future**: Unknown - gone from Hogwarts, destination unclear

### Minerva McGonagall [Ch. 120-122]

**Status**: Becomes Headmistress of Hogwarts [IMPLIED]

**Promotion**:
- Succeeds Dumbledore as Headmistress (timing unclear)
- Retains role as Head of Gryffindor [IMPLIED]
- Continues teaching Transfiguration
- Must manage Harry, Hermione, and Draco in Year Two

### Quirinus Quirrell / Lord Voldemort [Ch. 113, 120-122]

**Status**: Dead - permanently destroyed

**Final Fate**:
- Body destroyed by Harry using partial Transfiguration [Ch. 113]
- Horcruxes destroyed (Pioneer plaque sent into space, destroyed)
- Spirit/consciousness permanently ended
- No possibility of return

**Public Story**:
- Quirrell believed dead from "mysterious attacker"
- Voldemort believed finally defeated
- True story (Harry's victory) kept secret by Harry and allies

**Legacy**:
- Defence position remains cursed [IMPLIED]
- Impact on Harry's education and philosophy
- Changed Harry's understanding of death, consciousness, identity

## Magical Innovations and Discoveries

### True Patronus Charm [Ch. 45-46, throughout]

**Discovery**: Harry invented True Patronus (life and humanity vs. happy thought)

**Implications**:
- Can destroy Dementors permanently
- Vastly superior to animal Patronuses
- Harry plans to teach others eventually
- Potential to destroy all Dementors

**Secrecy**: Extent of True Patronus power kept partially secret

### Partial Transfiguration [Ch. 28, 113]

**Discovery**: Harry can Transfigure part of object, not whole

**Uses**:
- Defeated Voldemort (Transfigured part of gun to nanowire)
- Revolutionary magical advancement
- Breaks previously understood limits

**Secrecy**: Technique known to few, implications not fully public

### Hermione's Resurrection [Ch. 119]

**Method**: Philosopher's Stone + Transfiguration + Harry's ingenuity

**Result**:
- Hermione resurrected from death
- Transformed into unicorn Animagus
- Proves resurrection possible (under right conditions)

**Secrecy**: Method kept completely secret
- Public believes Hermione survived, not resurrected
- Only Harry, Hermione, and few allies know truth

### Unicorn Animagus [Ch. 119]

**Discovery**: Hermione first unicorn Animagus in history

**Implications**:
- Unprecedented magical achievement
- May grant unusual powers or limitations
- Challenges understanding of Animagus transformation
- Partially result of resurrection process

**Status**: Secret - only Harry and close allies know

## Long-Term Implications

### Harry's Plans

1. **Destroy All Dementors**: Active long-term goal [Ch. 121]
2. **Reform Magical Britain**: Via conspiracy with Hermione and Draco
3. **Achieve Immortality**: For self and eventually all humanity
4. **Advance Magical Science**: Systematic study of magic
5. **Prevent Future Dark Lords**: Reform system that created Voldemort

### Hermione's Plans

1. **Leverage House Granger**: Use Noble House status for reform
2. **Challenge Blood Purity**: Prove Muggle-borns equal through achievement
3. **Advance Knowledge**: Continue magical education and research
4. **Support Harry's Goals**: Partnership in conspiracy

### Draco's Plans

1. **Reform House Malfoy**: Transform from within when he inherits
2. **Reform Slytherin**: Change House culture toward rationality
3. **Support Conspiracy**: Work with Harry and Hermione despite political cost
