# Canon Divergences - How HPMOR Differs from Original Harry Potter

## Major Plot Divergences

### Timeline and Story Structure [EXPLICIT]

1. **Story Takes Place in Year One Only** [EXPLICIT]
   - **HPMOR**: Entire story (122 chapters) happens during Harry's first year at Hogwarts
   - **Canon**: Seven books covering seven years
   - **Implication**: Characters are 11 years old throughout, not aging to 17
   - **Writer warning**: Don't bring in plot elements from later canon years

2. **Story Ends with Voldemort's Final Defeat** [Ch. 113-122]
   - **HPMOR**: Voldemort defeated permanently at end of Year One
   - **Canon**: Voldemort defeated at end of Year Seven
   - **HPMOR specifics**: Harry outsmarts Voldemort in final confrontation, kills him and 36 Death Eaters
   - **Implication**: No Horcrux hunt storyline (different mechanics in HPMOR anyway)

3. **Hermione Dies and Is Resurrected** [Ch. 89, 122]
   - **HPMOR**: Hermione killed by troll (false-flag attack), preserved by Harry, resurrected
   - **Canon**: Hermione survives all seven books
   - **HPMOR specifics**: Becomes "like a unicorn" after resurrection [Ch. 122]
   - **Implication**: Hermione has died and returned; this transforms her

4. **No Horcrux Hunt** [Ch. 108]
   - **HPMOR**: Voldemort created 100+ improved Horcruxes, hidden in impossible-to-find locations (Pioneer plaque in space, Mariana Trench, Earth's mantle, etc.)
   - **Canon**: Seven traditional Horcruxes in findable locations
   - **Implication**: Can't adapt canon's Horcrux hunt plot

### Harry's Background [EXPLICIT]

5. **Raised by Loving Scientist Parents** [Ch. 1-6]
   - **HPMOR**: Michael Verres-Evans (scientist stepfather) and transformed Petunia raised Harry in loving, intellectual Oxford household
   - **Canon**: Abused by Vernon and Petunia Dursley, raised in cupboard under stairs
   - **Implication**: Harry is confident, well-adjusted, educated in science and rationality

6. **Petunia Beautiful and Loving** [Ch. 1-6]
   - **HPMOR**: Petunia drank beautification potion, married scientist, became better person
   - **Canon**: Bitter, cruel, resents magic
   - **Implication**: Harry has supportive mother figure who understands (some) magic

7. **Educated in Science and Rationality** [Ch. 1-6]
   - **HPMOR**: Has read Feynman Lectures on Physics, understands Bayesian reasoning, knows cognitive biases, trained in scientific method from age 5
   - **Canon**: Minimal education, no scientific background
   - **Implication**: Harry approaches magic scientifically, not instinctively

## Character Differences [EXPLICIT]

### Harry Potter

8. **Rationalist Genius vs. Reluctant Hero** [Throughout]
   - **HPMOR**: Brilliant rationalist, applies scientific method, wants to revolutionize magical world
   - **Canon**: Instinctive, emotional, just wants normal life
   - **Key difference**: "How does this work?" vs. "Just accept it"

9. **Proactive vs. Reactive** [Throughout]
   - **HPMOR**: Initiates experiments, questions everything, drives plot
   - **Canon**: Reacts to events, follows Dumbledore's guidance
   - **Key difference**: Harry makes plans vs. plans happen to Harry

10. **Rejects Death vs. Accepts It** [Ch. 39, 45, 96]
    - **HPMOR**: "Death is the enemy"; central quest to defeat death
    - **Canon**: Accepts death as "next great adventure" (like Dumbledore)
    - **Key difference**: Fundamental philosophical opposition

### Hermione Granger

11. **Ravenclaw vs. Gryffindor** [Ch. 12]
    - **HPMOR**: Sorted into Ravenclaw (intellectual house)
    - **Canon**: Gryffindor (brave house)
    - **Implication**: Different house dynamics, friend group

12. **Proactive Leader vs. Supportive Friend** [Throughout]
    - **HPMOR**: Founds S.P.H.E.W., leads army as General Sunshine, drives change
    - **Canon**: Supports Harry and Ron, follows their lead mostly
    - **Key difference**: Leadership vs. support role

13. **Dies and Is Resurrected** [Ch. 89, 122]
    - **HPMOR**: Killed by troll, brought back by Harry
    - **Canon**: Survives entire series
    - **Implication**: Has experienced death; transformed by resurrection

### Draco Malfoy

14. **Redeemable vs. Pure Villain** [Throughout]
    - **HPMOR**: Complex, capable of growth, learns scientific method, genuine friendship with Harry
    - **Canon**: Irredeemable bully, minor redemption only at very end
    - **Key difference**: Character arc vs. static antagonist

15. **Scientist Potential vs. Just Bully** [Ch. 7-8, throughout]
    - **HPMOR**: "You could have been a scientist" - genuine intellectual ability
    - **Canon**: No intellectual interests shown
    - **Key difference**: Harry recognizes and cultivates Draco's intelligence

16. **Mother Restored** [Ch. 120]
    - **HPMOR**: Narcissa was Obliviated into "Nancy Manson" in Australia, restored to Draco
    - **Canon**: Narcissa always present, defects to save Draco
    - **Implication**: Family reunion after trauma

### Professor Quirrell / Voldemort

17. **Competent Rationalist vs. Madman** [Throughout]
    - **HPMOR**: Terrifyingly intelligent, rational, effective strategist
    - **Canon**: Makes stupid mistakes, cartoonishly evil
    - **Key difference**: Worthy opponent vs. plot-device villain

18. **Brilliant Teacher vs. Incompetent Facade** [Ch. 16+]
    - **HPMOR**: Genuinely teaches practical Battle Magic, inspires students
    - **Canon**: Weak, stuttering, pretends incompetence
    - **Key difference**: Real mentor (though evil) vs. pure disguise

19. **Complex Relationship with Harry** [Throughout]
    - **HPMOR**: Genuine mentor elements, recognizes Harry as equal, wants Harry to rule Britain
    - **Canon**: Pure antagonism, wants to kill Harry
    - **Key difference**: Layered dynamic vs. simple enmity

20. **Was David Monroe (War Hero)** [Ch. 95, 108]
    - **HPMOR**: Identity as David Monroe, war hero who fought Voldemort, then became Voldemort through Horcrux possession
    - **Canon**: No David Monroe identity
    - **Implication**: Complex history, multiple identities

### Albus Dumbledore

21. **Competent But Flawed vs. Omniscient** [Throughout]
    - **HPMOR**: Makes real mistakes, can be wrong, not all-knowing
    - **Canon**: Seems to know everything, plan everything
    - **Key difference**: Human limitations vs. plot-device wisdom

22. **Manipulative vs. Purely Good** [Throughout]
    - **HPMOR**: Consciously manipulates for "greater good," morally grey
    - **Canon**: Manipulative but portrayed more sympathetically
    - **Key difference**: Story questions his methods, doesn't fully endorse them

23. **Fawkes Abandons Him** [Ch. 62-63]
    - **HPMOR**: Fawkes eventually chooses Harry over Dumbledore
    - **Canon**: Fawkes stays loyal until Dumbledore's death
    - **Implication**: Phoenix judgment finds Dumbledore wanting

### Minerva McGonagall

24. **Independent Thinker vs. Dumbledore Loyalist** [Throughout]
    - **HPMOR**: Questions Dumbledore, makes own decisions, goes "off-script" post-tragedy
    - **Canon**: Loyal deputy, rarely questions Headmaster
    - **Key difference**: Agency and independent judgment

25. **Becomes Headmistress** [Ch. 122]
    - **HPMOR**: Takes over as Headmistress while Dumbledore still alive
    - **Canon**: Becomes Headmistress only after Dumbledore's death
    - **Implication**: Leadership transition happens differently

### Severus Snape

26. **Eventually Escapes vs. Dies Tragically** [Ch. 121]
    - **HPMOR**: Resigns, takes new identity, starts new life, finds freedom
    - **Canon**: Dies protecting Harry, never escapes his past
    - **Key difference**: Liberation vs. tragic sacrifice

27. **Less Obsessed with Lily** [Ch. 121]
    - **HPMOR**: "Lily's killer is vanquished. I am content." - can move on
    - **Canon**: Obsession defines him until death
    - **Key difference**: Capable of growth and moving forward

## Magic System Differences [EXPLICIT]

### Unique to HPMOR

28. **Partial Transfiguration** [Ch. 28-29]
    - **HPMOR**: Can Transfigure part of object by thinking atomically
    - **Canon**: Must Transfigure "whole objects"
    - **Mechanism**: Understanding physics at atomic level enables exploit
    - **Implication**: Deep understanding changes what's possible

29. **True Patronus Can Destroy Dementors** [Ch. 45]
    - **HPMOR**: Human-shaped Patronus powered by rejection of death can permanently destroy Dementors
    - **Canon**: Animal-shaped Patronus can only repel Dementors
    - **Mechanism**: Understanding Dementors as Death's shadow, not fear-beings
    - **Implication**: Dementors can be eliminated, not just driven off

30. **Dementors as "Wounds in the World"** [Ch. 45]
    - **HPMOR**: Dementors are shadows of Death, wounds in reality
    - **Canon**: Dementors are creatures that feed on happiness
    - **Implication**: Ontologically different - not alive, can't be killed by normal means

### Modified Mechanics

31. **Time-Turner Rules More Explicit** [Ch. 17, 61]
    - **HPMOR**: Can't send information from future (enforced by magic), creates stable time loops, 6-hour limit explicitly explained
    - **Canon**: Rules vague, seems more flexible
    - **Implication**: Time travel paradoxes prevented by magic itself

32. **Interdict of Merlin Explained** [Ch. 23]
    - **HPMOR**: Powerful magic can't be taught through books/writing, only personal instruction
    - **Canon**: Not mentioned (or different mechanism)
    - **Implication**: Knowledge loss over generations, limits on magical education

33. **Source of Magic as Atlantean System** [Ch. 25, 108]
    - **HPMOR**: Magic is engineered system created by ancient Atlantis, uses DNA authentication
    - **Canon**: Magic is natural phenomenon, no origin explained
    - **Implication**: Magic can potentially be understood, modified, hacked

34. **Improved Horcrux System** [Ch. 108]
    - **HPMOR**: Voldemort created improved version requiring 100+ murders but enabling swift return, incorporated Resurrection Stone, locations Obliviated from his own memory
    - **Canon**: Traditional Horcruxes (7 of them) in findable locations
    - **Implication**: Can't be defeated by finding and destroying Horcruxes

### Different Spell Mechanics

35. **Wingardium Leviosa Requires Understanding** [Ch. 22]
    - **HPMOR**: "The universe actually did want you to say 'Wingardium Leviosa'" - spells have specific requirements
    - **Canon**: Less emphasis on why specific incantations matter
    - **Implication**: Magic has consistent rules worth understanding

36. **Broomsticks Use Aristotelian Physics** [Ch. 59]
    - **HPMOR**: Explicitly noted that broomstick flight follows Aristotelian not Newtonian physics
    - **Canon**: Physics of flight not examined
    - **Implication**: Magic modifies physical laws in specific ways

## World Differences [EXPLICIT]

### Political and Social

37. **House Granger as First Muggle-born Noble House** [Ch. 122]
    - **HPMOR**: Hermione made head of new Noble House Granger by Harry
    - **Canon**: No such house exists
    - **Implication**: Political revolution, precedent for Muggle-born elevation

38. **Wizengamot Procedure Detailed** [Ch. 80-84]
    - **HPMOR**: Blood debt law, Most Ancient Hall voting procedures, explicit corruption
    - **Canon**: Less detailed procedural information
    - **Implication**: Political system more thoroughly explored

39. **Student Armies at Hogwarts** [Ch. 21+]
    - **HPMOR**: Chaos Legion, Dragon Army, Sunshine Regiment - formal Battle Magic training
    - **Canon**: Dumbledore's Army (later, secret, Harry-led)
    - **Implication**: Different student dynamics and leadership opportunities

### Institutional

40. **Hogwarts Board of Governors More Active** [Ch. 98]
    - **HPMOR**: Board passes Educational Decrees, deploys Aurors to Hogwarts, creates student committees
    - **Canon**: Board mentioned but less active role
    - **Implication**: More institutional checks on Headmaster power

41. **Azkaban First Escape in History** [Ch. 56-62]
    - **HPMOR**: Bellatrix breakout is first successful escape ever (using rocket-powered broomstick and partial Transfiguration)
    - **Canon**: Sirius escaped before Bellatrix breakout
    - **Implication**: Azkaban genuinely considered inescapable

### Background

42. **Petunia and Lily's Relationship Different** [Ch. 5]
    - **HPMOR**: Lily rejected Petunia first when offered one-shot beautification potion
    - **Canon**: Petunia rejected Lily out of jealousy over magic
    - **Implication**: Blame more complex, both sisters at fault

43. **James Potter's Character** [Ch. 42, 96]
    - **HPMOR**: Part of Potter family quest to defeat death, "The last enemy that shall be destroyed is death"
    - **Canon**: No such family quest mentioned
    - **Implication**: Harry inheriting multi-generational mission

## Thematic Differences [IMPLIED]

### Core Philosophy

44. **Rationality vs. Love as Power** [Throughout]
    - **HPMOR**: Rationality, scientific method, clear thinking are powers
    - **Canon**: Love is the power Voldemort knows not
    - **Key difference**: Intellectual vs. emotional focus (though HPMOR has both)

45. **Death as Enemy vs. Teacher** [Ch. 39, 45]
    - **HPMOR**: Death is enemy to be defeated, not natural or acceptable
    - **Canon**: Death is "next great adventure," natural part of life
    - **Key difference**: Fundamental philosophical opposition to canon's acceptance

46. **Scientific Understanding vs. Mystery** [Throughout]
    - **HPMOR**: Magic should be understood, not accepted as mysterious
    - **Canon**: Some magic is simply mysterious and wonderful
    - **Key difference**: Reductionism vs. acceptance of wonder

## What Hasn't Changed (Common Mistakes)

### Keep These Canon Elements

47. **Hogwarts Houses Still Exist**: Gryffindor, Slytherin, Ravenclaw, Hufflepuff [Ch. 12]

48. **Basic Magical Creatures**: Dragons, unicorns, phoenixes, Dementors exist (even if mechanics differ) [Throughout]

49. **Basic Spell Names**: Wingardium Leviosa, Expecto Patronum, Avada Kedavra, etc. mostly unchanged [Throughout]

50. **Diagon Alley, Gringotts, Basic Locations**: Still exist with same names [Ch. 2, throughout]

51. **Quidditch Exists** (though Harry wants to eliminate Snitch): Game is still played [Ch. 35]

## Implications for Fanfiction Writers

### Critical Rules

**DO**:
- Remember story takes place in Year One only
- Harry is rationalist scientist, not instinctive hero
- Hermione is proactive leader, died and was resurrected
- Draco is redeemable, has real friendship with Harry
- Quirrell is competent Voldemort, brilliant teacher
- Partial Transfiguration requires physics understanding
- True Patronus can destroy Dementors
- Source of Magic is Atlantean system
- Death is the enemy (not accepted)

**DON'T**:
- Bring in canon plot elements from years 2-7
- Make Harry act like canon Harry (emotional, instinctive)
- Treat Hermione as support character
- Make Draco irredeemable villain
- Use traditional Horcrux mechanics (HPMOR's are different)
- Forget Hermione died and was resurrected
- Ignore that Voldemort is permanently defeated
- Make Dumbledore omniscient

### Common Contamination Points

**Watch Out For**:
1. **Year progression**: Story doesn't continue to Year Two
2. **Character ages**: Everyone is 11 throughout
3. **Hermione's status**: She's resurrected, has unicorn-like properties
4. **Voldemort's fate**: Permanently dead, no return possible
5. **Horcrux hunt**: Doesn't happen, different mechanics
6. **Ron's role**: Less prominent in HPMOR (not in trio)
7. **Harry's upbringing**: Loving, educated, not abused
8. **Snape's ending**: Escapes and lives, not tragic death
